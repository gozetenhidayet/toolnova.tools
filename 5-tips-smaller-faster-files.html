5 Tips to Keep Your Files Small and Fast to Share

4 min read · Updated July 2026

Large files slow everything down — uploads take longer, emails bounce, and whoever you're sending them to has to wait. None of these habits take extra effort once they're routine.

1 Resize images before you insert them

A photo straight from a phone can be 3000+ pixels wide — far more than any screen needs. Before adding an image to a document or PDF, resize it to roughly the size it'll actually be displayed at. You'll barely notice the quality difference, but the file size drops dramatically.

2 Compress PDFs before sending, not after they bounce

Get in the habit of compressing PDFs before you attach them anywhere, especially ones with scanned pages or screenshots. A 30-second compression step avoids the back-and-forth of "your file was too large."

3 Export scans at a reasonable resolution

When scanning documents, 300 DPI is plenty for anything that'll be read on a screen or printed at normal size. Scanning at 600 DPI "just in case" quadruples the file size for detail nobody will use.

4 Split huge documents instead of sending the whole thing

If you only need to share a few pages from a much larger PDF, split out just those pages rather than sending everything. It's faster for you to send and faster for the other person to open.

5 Use the right format for the job

Don't convert an image to PDF just to email it — send the image directly. Don't export a simple one-page note as a high-res scan. Matching the format and quality to what's actually needed avoids bloated files from the start.

Put these into practice — free, and everything stays in your browser.
