How PDF to Word conversion actually works

A PDF that was created directly from a word processor or design tool contains a real, invisible text layer underneath the visual page — every letter, its font, its position, and its formatting are recorded as data, not just pixels. Converting that kind of PDF to Word means reading that text layer and rebuilding it as proper Word paragraphs, headings, and formatting, which is why the result is usually clean and immediately editable.

A scanned PDF is fundamentally different — it's really just a photograph of a page saved inside a PDF wrapper. There's no text layer at all, only a flat image. If you try to convert a scanned PDF with a standard converter, you'll get an empty or garbled result, because there's no text for the converter to find. Scanned documents need OCR (Optical Character Recognition) first, which reads the pixels and reconstructs the text before any conversion can happen.

A simple way to tell which type you have: open the PDF and try to select a word with your cursor. If you can select and highlight individual words, it's a real text-based PDF and this converter will work well. If clicking just selects the whole page like an image, it's scanned, and you'll want our Scan PDF to Text tool first.

Frequently asked questions

Is PDF to Word really free?

Yes — completely free with no signup, no watermark, and no artificial limits beyond your device's own memory.

Is my file uploaded to a server?

No. The conversion runs entirely in your browser using local JavaScript. Your file is processed on your own device and never sent anywhere.

Will my PDF's exact formatting be preserved?

Text, paragraphs, headings and basic formatting like bold and italics convert reliably. Complex layouts — multi-column magazine designs, heavily styled tables, or unusual fonts — may need minor manual cleanup after conversion.

Does this work on scanned PDFs?

This converter reads the existing text layer inside a PDF. A scanned PDF has no text layer, only a flat image, so it needs OCR first — try our Scan PDF to Text tool for that case.

Will tables convert correctly?

Simple tables usually convert with their row and column structure intact. Tables with merged cells or unusual spacing may need a quick manual adjustment afterward.

Is there a file size limit?

There's no artificial limit imposed by the tool — the practical limit depends on your device's own memory, since conversion happens locally.

Does PDF to Word work on mobile?

Yes, it works on any modern mobile browser, including uploading files directly from your phone's storage.

Can I convert a password-protected PDF?

You'll need to remove the password protection from the PDF first (using a PDF editor that supports it) before this tool can read and convert its contents.
