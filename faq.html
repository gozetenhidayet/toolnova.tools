Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about how toolnova.tools works, your privacy, and getting the most out of our tools.

Are these tools really free?

Yes. Every tool on toolnova.tools is completely free to use, with no signup, no watermarks, and no hidden "pro" paywall. The site is supported by non-intrusive advertising.

Do I need to create an account?

No account is required for any tool. Just open a tool and use it — nothing is saved to a profile because there is no profile.

Is my data safe? Do you store my files?

Most tools (PDF conversion, merging, compression, calculators, generators) run entirely inside your browser. Your files are processed on your own device and are never uploaded to our servers or seen by us.

Why did OCR or text extraction come out with errors?

OCR accuracy depends heavily on image quality. Low-resolution, tilted, or handwritten scans produce more errors than clean, high-contrast printed text. Try re-scanning at a higher resolution for better results.

Can I use these tools on my phone?

Yes, toolnova.tools is fully mobile-responsive and every tool works on modern mobile browsers, including uploading files from your phone's storage or camera.

Why is my converted file's formatting slightly different?

Complex layouts, unusual fonts, or embedded objects can shift slightly during conversion. Simple documents (letters, resumes, reports) convert with very high fidelity; heavily designed documents may need minor manual cleanup.

Can I use toolnova.tools for commercial purposes?

Yes, our tools can be used for personal or business use, subject to our Terms of Service.

Do you support languages other than English?

The interface can be automatically translated into dozens of languages using the language selector in the top navigation, powered by Google Translate.

How do I report a bug or request a new tool?

Use our Contact page — we read every message and prioritize frequently requested tools and reported issues.

Is there a file size limit?

Since most tools run in your browser, the practical limit depends on your device's memory rather than a fixed cap. Very large files (500MB+) may process more slowly on older devices.
